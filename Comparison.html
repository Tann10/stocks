Price to Sales ratio Price to sales ratio = Current Share Price / Sales per Share Higher the P/S ratio, higher is the valuation of the firm. One has to compare the P/S ratio with its competitors in the industry to get a fair sense of how expensive or cheap the stock is.

Observations ~ Apple P/S is 3.46 that means for every dollar of sales, the stock price is 3.46 times higher. ~ In SnP 500 stock, nvidia has highest P/S ratio compared with companies in the same sector.

Price to Book ratio Price to book ratio = Current Share Price / Book Value per Share The higher the ratio, the more expensive the stock is. A high ratio could indicate the firm is overvalued relative to the equity/ book value of the company. A low ratio could indicate the company is undervalued relative to the equity/ book value of the company.

Observations ~ Apple P/B is 5.66 that means the stock price is 5.66 times higher than its book value. ~ Intuit, Netflix, Mastercard have high book value compared to other companies in the IT sector. ~ If this ratio is around 1 then investor considers it as a solid investment.

Price to Earnings ratio Price to earnings ratio = Current Share Price / earnings per Share
In general, a high P/E suggests that investors are expecting higher earnings growth in the future compared to companies with a lower P/E. A low P/E can indicate either that a company may currently be undervalued or that the company is doing exceptionally well relative to its past trends. When a company has no earnings or is posting losses, in both cases P/E will be expressed as “N/A.” Though it is possible to calculate a negative P/E, this is not the common convention.

Observations ~ Apple P/E is 16.9 every unit of profit generated by APPLE, the market participants are willing to pay $ 16.9 to acquire the share.
~ Salesforce has highest P/E ratio ~ 520.2
